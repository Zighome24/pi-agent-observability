We want to expand the Steelman agent's visual vocabulary with a family of three new artifact types so it can make the bear case land faster and harder. (1) A quote artifact — as an investor, when the bear case turns on something a CEO admitted on an earnings call, a short-seller wrote in a published report, or a regulator said on the record, I want that exact line rendered as a typography-forward callout in the artifacts pane: a large opening quote mark, the quote set in italic, and a small attribution line below showing the speaker, their role, the date, and an inline source link, so I can see the receipt at a glance and click straight through. Also leave room for commentary to convey the bear thesis. (2) A catalyst-timeline artifact — when the bear thesis hinges on a chain of dated events (earnings miss → guidance cut → covenant breach → ratings downgrade), I want those plotted on a left-to-right timeline with each node labeled, dated, and color-coded by severity (bull/neutral/bear) so I can see the domino order and pacing at a glance instead of reconstructing it from prose. (3) A valuation-gauge artifact — I want a single horizontal range bar showing bear / base / bull case price (or multiple) markers and a "where it trades today" indicator so I can instantly tell whether the stock is priced for perfection, priced fairly, or already pricing in the bear case, without parsing paragraphs of analyst targets.
